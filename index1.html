Multi-Function Variable Digital Clock

Designed a digital clock with ATMEGA4809, programmed with C++, on a PCB with settings that allow for 12/24-hour basis display in real-time as well as the ability to serve as a timer/counter using four 7-segment displays, and simultaneously act as a battery charger for other devices.

Background

ESE 123 - Introduction to Computer and Electrical Engineering is one of the first courses taken by undergraduates planning to study the Computer Engineering or Electrical Engineering major at Stony Brook University. The common project that the students work on throughout the semester is the multi-function variable digital clock. The project is designed and prototyped by professor David Westerfeld and the students are required to finish the design and implementation of its functionality through the weekly labs. The project is designated to be a multi-function digital clock that can be used as a timer, counter, and battery charger.

What It Does

The final design of the digital clock is programmed to have three simulataneous functionalities. There are three pushbutton switches labelled UP, OK, and DOWN used to navigate through the different settings and options. The first functionality is to display the time in real-time, with the option to swap between 12-hour and 24-hour basis using four seven-segment display modules and the pushbuttons. The second functionality is to act as a charger with a USB A port to other devices and keep track of the delivered power to that device. The third functionality is to act as a timer or counter with options to pause, reset, and restart the timer. The timer can be set to a maximum of 99 minutes and 59 seconds before rolling over to 0. Of course with the ATMEGA4809, the digital clock can be programmed to have other functionalities and features.
